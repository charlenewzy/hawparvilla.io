Immerse yourself in the laid-back vibes of Haw Par Villa. Lucky ones will even spot our squirrels leaping playfully from tree to tree. Transform the space for any occasion – the sky’s the limit!

Culture Courtyard

Specially curated displays provide comparative insights that will give visitors a better understanding of the commonalities across the world’s major belief systems – and how different communities around the world, as well as in Singapore, draw meaning from death and dying.

Stretched over 3,800 sqm, Hell’s Museum also provides a deeper understanding of Haw Par Villa’s infamous 10 Courts of Hell. Discover how concepts behind the graphic representations of sins and punishments in the afterlife evolved as ancient civilisations shared their cultures across time and space. Visit us for an eye-opening experience and have a hell of a time!

Exhibitions

These captivating exhibitions lays bare the history of the breath-taking Haw Par Villa and the fascinating ideas and concepts inspired by it.

BEYOND THE VEIL

Challenging traditional views on subjects surrounding and beyond death, Beyond the Veil brings together four contemporary artists that explore the unsettling topic through various imaginings as inspired by current situations with the use of new media and interdisciplinary approaches.

THE HAW PAR VILLA STORY

Haw Par Villa – formerly known as the Tiger Balm Garden – is an 8.5-hectare Asian cultural park, the last of its kind in the world. Learn more about this breath-taking park at the Jade House!

Educational Programmes

Be an art critic for the day and get your creativity flowing with our interactive programme!

Learn about the garden’s creator, Aw Boon Haw, and how he used cultural art to teach valuable lessons to society. Embark on an amazing art journey through Haw Par Villa and pick your way through a remarkable landscape filled to the brim with sculpture of all sorts.

Through a labyrinth of colorful stone and elaborate dioramas, famous Chinese classics such as Journey to the West and Romance of the Three Kingdoms, and the story of the Eight Immortals Crossing the Eastern Sea are brought to life in stunning detail. Discover also how religions, cultures, and artistic styles came together to influence Chinese and Southeast Asian art and architecture!

Students will be tasked to examine primary and secondary sources, the sculptures in the park and the stories they heard from the facilitator. They will use the knowledge they have gathered to complete a creative writing activity.
